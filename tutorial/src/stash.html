Set the version to 0.0.0 in case you are first just playing around for a bit. Semvar and npm is a bit lost on this "playing around" case, since technically anyone could rely on your package the second you publish it to npm, but it's still better than publishing it with 1.0.0 following semvar, and being at version 15.0.0 after two days of playing around. Alternatively you can not publish it to npm and use npm pack locally. In any case, lets stick with the 0.0.0 here.
